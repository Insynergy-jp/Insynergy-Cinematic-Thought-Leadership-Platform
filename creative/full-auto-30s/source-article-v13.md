# The Missing Approval Boundary

An autonomous marketing system can analyze campaigns, negotiate with vendors, purchase assets, increase budgets, and launch distribution exactly as instructed. None of those actions has to be technically wrong for the outcome to become a governance failure.

The failure begins when an organization enables full autonomy without defining the point at which a machine must return judgment to a human. A late emergency stop cannot repair commitments that have already been made, and monitoring cannot decide what amount of spend requires approval.

The required design is explicit: a budget limit, spending authority, approval events, escalation ownership, and a decision boundary. The AI did exactly what it was told. No one designed when approval should begin.

Decision Design does not merely stop AI. It designs where AI must stop and ask.
