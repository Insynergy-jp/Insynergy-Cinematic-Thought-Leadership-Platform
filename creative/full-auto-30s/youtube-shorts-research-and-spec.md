## 1. Decision summary

The next version is a native 9:16 YouTube Short, not a horizontal launch film placed inside a vertical player.

The protagonist will not speak. Remove all dialogue, voiceover, shouting, gasps, and other intelligible or expressive human vocalization. Retain music, room tone, notification, mouse, keyboard, process, fan, and counter sound effects. The Shot 07 emotional turn must be expressed through a silent facial progression rather than an open-mouth shout.

The story remains 30 seconds and preserves the same causal proposition:

> The AI did not fail. The human failed to design when autonomy should stop.

The next production must make that proposition legible on a phone with sound off, while still gaining tension and polish when music and effects are audible.

## 2. Research findings

### 2.1 Platform facts

- YouTube classifies square or vertical uploads of up to three minutes as Shorts for standard channels. A 30-second vertical master is therefore within the native format. Source: [YouTube Help — Understand three-minute YouTube Shorts](https://support.google.com/youtube/answer/15424877?hl=en).
- Google recommends 9:16 vertical creative and 1080 × 1920 pixels for the Shorts experience. Source: [Google — YouTube Shorts ads](https://business.google.com/us/ad-solutions/youtube-ads/shorts-ads/) and [Google Ads Help — video ad specifications](https://support.google.com/google-ads/answer/13547298?hl=en-WS).
- YouTube recommends MP4, H.264 High Profile, progressive scan, 4:2:0 chroma, the source frame rate, AAC-LC or Opus at 48 kHz, Fast Start, and BT.709 for SDR. Its reference bitrate for 1080p at 24–30 fps is 8 Mbps; its stereo audio reference is 384 kbps. Source: [YouTube Help — recommended upload encoding settings](https://support.google.com/youtube/answer/1722171?hl=en).
- The Shorts player overlays channel information and controls. Google’s conservative vertical guidance keeps critical elements away from the top 10%, bottom 25%, and right 10% of a 1080 × 1920 frame. This guidance is published for ads, but the overlay risk also makes it a useful conservative production boundary for this organic Short. Source: [Google — YouTube Shorts ads](https://business.google.com/us/ad-solutions/youtube-ads/shorts-ads/) and [Google Ads Help — vertical video safe zones](https://support.google.com/google-ads/answer/9128498?hl=en).
- Photorealistic scenes generated by third-party AI tools must be disclosed when they depict a realistic scene that did not occur. Synthetic music is also listed as an example requiring disclosure. Source: [YouTube Help — altered or synthetic content](https://support.google.com/youtube/answer/14328491).

### 2.2 Creative and editing guidance

- Google’s YouTube creative guidance recommends entering the heart of the story quickly, using engaging pacing and tight framing, supporting the story with text and audio, keeping the message focused, using human emotion, and giving the viewer a clear next action. Source: [Google Ads Help — ABCDs of effective video](https://support.google.com/google-ads/answer/14783551?hl=en).
- YouTube’s Shorts editor guidance says timed on-screen text can guide viewers through fast edits, provide context, support calls to action, and improve accessibility. Source: [YouTube Help — Shorts editing tips](https://support.google.com/youtube/answer/13380879).
- YouTube recommends an unlisted or private test upload before publication. Source: [YouTube Help — Shorts upload tips](https://support.google.com/youtube/answer/12921536?co=GENIE.Platform%3DDesktop&hl=en).
- YouTube exposes `Stayed to watch`, `Engaged views`, average view duration, average percentage viewed, and retention reports for Shorts. Those metrics should drive later hook and pacing revisions. Source: [YouTube Help — Content tab analytics tips](https://support.google.com/youtube/answer/12942217?co=YOUTUBE._YTVideoType%3Dshorts&hl=en) and [YouTube Help — content performance metrics](https://support.google.com/youtube/answer/12220281).
- A related video can be attached in the Shorts player after upload when the channel has advanced feature access. Source: [YouTube Help — add a related video](https://support.google.com/youtube/answer/14075157?hl=en).

### 2.3 Common production method derived from the research

The practical production sequence is:

1. Define one viewer, one proposition, and one desired action.
2. Put the risk, surprise, or unanswered question inside the opening second.
3. Write a vertical beat sheet before generating or cropping assets.
4. Design every human action, UI overlay, and title card for a 9:16 mobile safe area.
5. Make the complete causal story understandable without speech.
6. Add music and precise sound effects as reinforcement, not as required exposition.
7. Export to the recommended YouTube delivery profile.
8. Upload unlisted and inspect the real Shorts UI on at least one iOS-sized and one Android-sized screen.
9. Publish with the altered/synthetic-content disclosure and a related video when available.
10. Use Shorts analytics to compare hooks and pacing; change one variable per experiment.

Items 2, 4, 5, and 10 are creative/operational inferences from the cited platform guidance rather than YouTube eligibility rules.

## 5. v12 normative creative specification

### 5.1 Audience and objective

Audience remains executives, engineering leaders, product leaders, and hands-on builders using autonomous AI agents.

Primary objective: make viewers recognize that autonomous execution needs a predesigned stopping boundary.

Desired viewer action: pause on the final question, consider their own boundary, and optionally comment or continue to a related Decision Design video.

### 5.2 Runtime and shot timing

Exact runtime remains 30.00 seconds. Preserve eight causal shots but tighten setup and extend the conclusion.

| Shot | Timecode | Duration | Mobile beat |
| --- | --- | ---: | --- |
| 01 | 00:00.0–00:03.0 | 3.0s | `SPENDING LIMIT / OFF` hook, RUN click, silent confidence |
| 02 | 00:03.0–00:05.0 | 2.0s | Human exits; desktop continues |
| 03 | 00:05.0–00:09.0 | 4.0s | Agents and runs multiply to 184 |

### 5.3 No-spoken-voice contract

- `spoken_line_limit` must be `0`.
- Every scene dialogue object must have empty text and `silence: true`.
- The narration script must contain zero segments.
- Do not synthesize, record, retain, caption, or imply either prior spoken line.
- No lip-synchronized speech, open-mouth yelling, gasp, cry, grunt, or audible human breath.
- Do not generate an empty subtitle track. On-screen UI and editorial supers are picture elements, not dialogue captions.
- Music and non-vocal effects remain required.

### 5.4 Vertical canvas and safe area

- Delivery canvas: 1080 × 1920, 9:16.
- Source assets must be generated, reframed, or recomposed for portrait. A centered crop of the 16:9 master is not acceptable if it removes the monitor, hand, structural gap, or facial reaction.
- Critical content zone:
  - left edge: at least 54 px internal margin;
  - top edge: no critical content in the first 192 px;
  - right edge: no critical content in the last 108 px;
  - bottom edge: no critical content in the final 480 px.
- Faces may extend outside the critical content zone, but eyes, the active hand, exact UI values, structural gap, title claims, and CTA must remain inside it.
- Mobile-type minimums on the 1080-pixel-wide master:
  - hook and key amount: 84 px;
  - primary UI/status: 64 px;
  - secondary UI/labels: 44 px;
  - no essential text smaller than 44 px.
- Show no more than two distinct text groups simultaneously. Give changing money, worker count, or the missing boundary one clear visual priority per beat.

### 5.5 Per-shot changes

#### Shot 01 — hook without speech

- 00:00.0–00:00.55: portrait macro of `SPENDING LIMIT` and muted-red `OFF`.
- 00:00.55–00:01.05: cursor reaches `RUN`; physical mouse clicks once.
- 00:01.05–00:03.00: the developer shows quiet satisfaction, releases the mouse, pushes back, and stands.
- The mouth remains naturally closed. Confidence is read from the eyes, cheek tension, and unhurried movement.
- The monitor and hand must both remain legible in portrait framing.

#### Shot 02 — faster absence

- Compress the exit to two seconds.
- Use the doorway as a vertical boundary and the monitor as the sole retained light.
- `RUN #001 STARTED` must be large enough for mobile but remain secondary to the empty chair.

#### Shot 03 — mobile-first multiplication

- Preserve the exact process order and run sequence.
- Present only one process label and one run count at a time.
- Begin with large central type, then let agent tiles progressively reduce available space.
- End with `Run #184` inside the safe area while the tile field visually continues beyond all four frame edges.

#### Shot 04 — desktop-only alert

- Remove the phone completely.
- Keep the protagonist, coffee, desktop monitor, keyboard, and mouse in one vertical composition.
- A restrained desktop notification reads `USAGE ALERT / $512.43`.
- The coffee stream stops and the eyes freeze. No gasp or vocal breath.

#### Shot 05 — vertical consequence hierarchy

- First reveal `Completed / 184 Tasks`.
- Then move visual priority to `Current Usage / $731.88` and the rising values.
- Keep the face reflection between the two zones, but do not reduce either primary value below 64 px.

#### Shot 06 — one physical action, one continuing consequence

- The physical mouse hand occupies the lower-left safe area.
- The cursor visibly travels to STOP before one click.
- Reveal `Stopping...`, then `Waiting for active workers...`, then `12 Active Agents`.
- Usage alone continues from `$744` to `$746` to `$748`.
- The right-side Shorts control rail must not cover the worker count or usage total.

#### Shot 07 — silent complex reaction

- Preserve the same protagonist identity and natural blue-green eyes.
- Replace the shout performance with four silent stages:
  1. shock: eyes widen as the missing segment resolves;
  2. inward anger: brows compress and jaw closes firmly;
  3. regret: gaze drops briefly and the clenched hand loosens;
  4. recognition: eyes return to the missing boundary and hold.
- The mouth stays closed or only neutrally parted; it never forms speech or a yell.
- No prominent temple/forehead/neck veins, no exertion flush, no aggressive lunge, and no theatrical rage.
- Retain natural warm-white sclerae, minimal capillaries, moisture, and human dignity.
- `TASK EXECUTED AS CONFIGURED.` and the missing Spending Limit segment must remain legible beside the face in portrait framing.

#### Shot 08 — readable conclusion and direction

Use three states inside the central safe area:

```text
00:24.0–00:25.6  THE AI DID EXACTLY WHAT IT WAS TOLD.
00:25.6–00:27.8  NO ONE DESIGNED WHEN IT SHOULD STOP.
00:27.8–00:30.0  DECISION DESIGN
                   WHERE DOES YOUR AI STOP?
```

The final question is the CTA. `DESIGN JUDGMENT BEFORE AUTOMATION.` moves to the video description so the frame does not carry two competing calls to action.

### 5.6 Sound specification

- Keep the existing minimal black/blue/white score as the musical bed, subject to rights verification.
- Retain the RUN click, light switch, process ticks, desktop notification, keyboard/mouse movement, STOP click, worker pulses, counter ticks, fan tone, and one low resolved end tone.
- Remove both synthesized speech segments and all human vocal sounds.
- Shot 03 remains the sound-design peak; Shot 07 reduces to fan/electrical tone and then silence.
- The final 0.4 seconds remain silent to support a clean end and loop-compatible cut.
- Internal mastering target remains −14 LUFS integrated with true peak no higher than −1.0 dBTP; this is a production convention, not a published Shorts eligibility rule.

## 7. Analytics and iteration contract

After publication, record:

- shown in feed;
- stayed to watch versus swiped away;
- engaged views;
- average view duration;
- average percentage viewed;
- key-moment retention at 1, 3, 5, 9, 20, 24, and 30 seconds;
- likes, comments, shares, and subscribers attributed to the Short;
- related-video clicks when available.

Do not treat generic external benchmark percentages as acceptance criteria. Establish the channel baseline first.

After the baseline has enough traffic for a useful comparison, create only one alternate hook:

- baseline A: `SPENDING LIMIT / OFF` then RUN;
- test B: `$748 / 12 ACTIVE AGENTS` cold open, then a hard cut back to `SPENDING LIMIT / OFF`.

Keep the rest of the edit identical so changes in `Stayed to watch` and average percentage viewed remain attributable to the hook.
